📐 Algebra Adventure Form One

🔢 Master variables, expressions, and simple equations — step by step. Algebra is like a puzzle; learn the rules and solve anything!

🧠 1. What is Algebra?

Algebra uses letters (variables) to represent unknown numbers. Instead of saying "a number plus 3", we write x + 3.

✨ Examples:
• y + 5 → a number increased by 5
• 2n → twice a number
• a - 7 → seven less than a number
💡 Key fact: In algebra, we write multiplication without '×' → 3 × m becomes 3m.
Division: n ÷ 4 becomes n/4 or n/4.

📚 2. Like Terms & Simplifying

Like terms have the same variable raised to the same power.

✅ 3x + 5x = 8x    (both are 'x')
✅ 7y - 2y = 5y
⚠️ 4a + 3b cannot be simplified further (different letters).

📝 Simplify this expression:

🧮 5p + 2q + 3p - q = ?

⚖️ 3. Solving Simple Equations

We keep the equation balanced like a scale. To find the variable, do the opposite operation.

✔️ x + 7 = 12 → subtract 7 both sides → x = 5
✔️ 3y = 18 → divide both sides by 3 → y = 6
✔️ a - 4 = 9 → add 4 both sides → a = 13
🌟 Solve for t: t + 15 = 42
🌟 Solve for m: 4m = 36

✍️ 4. Words → Algebra

Turning real-life problems into algebra is a superpower!

🟢 "A number decreased by 8" → n - 8
🟢 "Double a number plus 5" → 2x + 5
🟢 "The sum of y and 12, divided by 3" → (y + 12)/3
📖 Translate into algebra: "Three times a number, increased by 10"
🧠 Always read carefully: "times" means multiply, "increased by" means add, "less than" is subtraction.
